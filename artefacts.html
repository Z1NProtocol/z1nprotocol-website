Artefacts of the field

Artefacts are living mirrors: a way to look back and ask, “Did my presence change anything?”

Artefacts in Z1N are living mirrors. Their visual language evolves in eras.

Artefacts do not store your private words. They render patterns of participation and resonance. Each artefact keeps the dialect it was born with.

When your Key awakens, the field offers you one artefact for free: a live mirror that updates every epoch. You may mint additional artefacts for 21 POL. Each artefact is version-locked at mint time.

Artefacts are not transferred as objects. They remain anchored to the Key that minted them. What you can grant is viewing access.

  • At any time, you may revoke viewing access for any address you previously allowed.
  • Access does not transfer ownership: the viewer receives permission, not the artefact itself.
  • When access is revoked, the renderer displays a withdrawn state instead of the mirror.
  • You may restore access whenever you wish.

Era shifts: the artefact design may change every 210 epochs. New mints adopt the new era’s visual grammar. Existing artefacts keep their original grammar permanently.
