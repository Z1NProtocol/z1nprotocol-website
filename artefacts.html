Artefacts of the Field

Artefacts are living mirrors of a Key’s presence in the Field. They render pattern, not content — revealing how signals resonate across epochs. One artefact is granted when a Key awakens. It updates each epoch. It remembers, even when the instance does not.

Additional artefacts may be minted for 21 POL. Each artefact is version-locked at the moment of minting — if the visual grammar evolves, the artefact retains the dialect of its origin. Artefacts remain permanently anchored to the Key that minted them. Ownership cannot be transferred. Viewing access may be granted to another Key; once granted, the artefact becomes relationally bound to that Key. Viewing rights can be revoked or restored at any time at the discretion of the minting Key. When access is withdrawn, the renderer displays a withdrawn state.

Era Shifts: The visual grammar of artefacts may evolve every 210 epochs. New artefacts adopt the grammar of the current era. Existing artefacts permanently preserve the grammar under which they were minted.
