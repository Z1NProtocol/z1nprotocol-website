The field is not ruled. It evolves. Every Key carries one voice. Governance exists to protect continuity, not to impose direction.

8.1 Voting Power

Each Key holds one vote. Genesis Keys hold two votes. Voting power is soulbound and cannot be delegated or transferred.

You may vote per Key individually or cast votes in batch. Each Key may vote only once per proposal.

8.2 Address Limits – Preventing Dominance

To prevent concentration of influence through a single wallet, an address may:

  • Submit a maximum of 2 proposals per epoch.
  • Cast a maximum of 10 votes per epoch.

Keys remain sovereign units of voice, but no single address may flood governance activity. This protects diversity of participation.

8.3 Two Types of Change

1. Parameter Adjustments

  • Adjustable values (e.g. layer weights, quorum range, timelock delay).
  • Voting window: 7 epochs.
  • Executed after timelock.

2. Protocol Upgrades

  • New logic, structural changes, migration.
  • Require an available upgrade slot.
  • Each slot may be used once and is permanently consumed.

The contract determines whether a proposal is a parameter change or an upgrade.

8.4 Upgrade Slots – Controlled Evolution

Every 210,000 Keys sold unlocks one upgrade slot. A maximum of 100 slots exist.

This limits structural mutation while allowing long-term evolution. Once all slots are used, the protocol becomes structurally fixed.
