Canons marked
Canon is permanent memory. Where an artefact is a relational bond between two Keys, a Canon Anchor is a Key marking an epoch as meaningful — forever. One anchor per Key per epoch, immutable once minted, carrying a short on-chain commitment (max 64 chars) and its hash: a readable declaration plus cryptographic proof. The smallest irreversible unit of memory in Z1N — a Key saying, without negotiation, "This mattered. I was here when it happened."
